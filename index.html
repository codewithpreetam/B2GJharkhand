The Crisis We Face

Jharkhand powers India.
Who powers Jharkhand?

For over a century, Jharkhand's coal has lit up Indian cities, fuelled industries, and built a modern economy. The communities living above those seams? They've inhaled the dust, lost their land, and buried their futures underground.

The air in Dhanbad registers PM2.5 levels that exceed WHO safe limits by 10 times. Children in mining towns grow up with compromised lungs. Rivers run black. Forests — once sacred, once livelihood — are blasted into rubble. And as the world pivots toward clean energy, these communities risk being left behind again: not with coal, but with nothing at all.

This is not an abstract climate story. This is home.

🫁

Air Toxicity

PM2.5 and SO₂ levels in coal belt towns pose severe risks to respiratory health — especially for children and the elderly.

💧

Contaminated Water

Acid mine drainage poisons local water bodies. Fluoride and heavy metal contamination affects drinking water for millions.

🌍

Displacement

Entire villages have been relocated with no consultation, no compensation parity, and no plan for what comes next.

📉

Economic Fragility

As coal declines, communities built around mines face mass unemployment with no green alternatives in sight.

Our Philosophy

Three pillars that keep
everything rooted

I

Community First

Every programme we run is designed with communities — not for them. We don't parachute in with solutions. We sit in courtyards, attend village meetings, and let lived experience shape our strategy. The people most affected by the coal economy are the most qualified to redesign it.

Participatory Design
II

Youth Led

The average age of our team is 24. We believe young people in coal communities are not passive victims of an energy crisis — they are its most powerful solution. We invest in young leaders, then get out of their way.

Next-Gen Leadership
III

Data + Storytelling

Numbers without stories are ignored. Stories without numbers are dismissed. We insist on both: rigorous environmental data paired with the human narratives that make decision-makers move. Evidence that moves people. People who create change.

Evidence-Driven Narrative
Impact Vision

Where we're going.
Why it matters globally.

Jharkhand is not unique. From Appalachia to the Ruhr Valley, from South Africa's Mpumalanga to Indonesia's Kalimantan — coal communities everywhere face the same impossible question: what comes next?

B2G Jharkhand is building a replicable model of community-led just transition — one that centres human dignity, ecological restoration, and economic reinvention. We are not waiting for policy to catch up. We are creating the proof of concept that policy will follow.

By 2030, we aim to engage 50,000 youth across Jharkhand's coal districts, establish 100 community air monitoring stations, and train 10,000 workers in green economy skills — while documenting the model for replication across Asia's coal belt.

2025

Foundation Phase

Establish monitoring networks across 5 core districts. Launch first youth cohort. Begin documentary series.

2026

Scale Phase

Expand to 12 districts. Launch green skills academy. First policy submissions to state government.

2028

Influence Phase

National model recognition. UN Climate conference representation. Cross-border knowledge exchange with global coal communities.

2030

Legacy Phase

50,000 youth engaged. Replication in 3 additional Indian coal states. Open-source transition toolkit released globally.
